Developing Conference Posters

As much as it is important to conduct research, it is also important to present it. That is the art of science—communicating your findings clearly and effectively to peers, collaborators, and the wider community. A well-designed conference poster not only highlights your research but also invites engagement and discussion, making your work accessible and memorable.

Collegiate Scientific Poster Resources

  • UMBC Undergraduate Research Poster Examples
    https://ur.umbc.edu/poster-presentation-examples/
    A rich collection of actual student posters across disciplines, showcasing diverse design approaches and research topics.
  • University of Texas at Austin Undergraduate Research Poster Samples
    https://undergradcollege.utexas.edu/academics/undergraduate-research/guide-creating-research-posters/poster-samples
    Samples with detailed notes on layout, content, and design effectiveness from UT undergraduates.
  • University of Illinois Urbana-Champaign Research Poster Examples
    https://guides.library.illinois.edu/poster/ex
    Examples highlighting good design principles and content organization for academic posters.
  • Cornell University Engineering Poster Design Guide
    https://www.engineering.cornell.edu/scientific-poster-design/
    A comprehensive guide with examples and best practices tailored for engineering research posters.
  • Michigan State University Undergraduate Research Abstracts & Posters
    https://urca.msu.edu/undergraduate-research
    Includes both abstracts and poster examples from undergraduate research projects, useful for seeing how research is presented visually and textually.
  • University of Washington Undergraduate Research Posters
    https://depts.washington.edu/uwurc/posters.html
    A gallery of posters from various disciplines, emphasizing clarity and professionalism in student research communication.
Note: The "Better Posters Blog" by Zen Faulkes is highly recommended by many university guides, but does not have a single, unique URL in the provided sources. However, searching for "Better Posters blog Zen Faulkes" will lead you to the official site. Also, Canva (canva.com) and Pixabay (pixabay.com) are frequently recommended for templates and free images.
